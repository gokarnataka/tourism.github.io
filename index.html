About Ballari Tourism

Ballari district in Karnataka is home to world-famous heritage sites and breathtaking natural landscapes. From the ancient ruins of Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, to the lush hills of Sandur, this region offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nature.

Hampi – The City of Ruins

Virupaksha Temple

One of the oldest functioning temples in India, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and a major spiritual center in Hampi.

Vittala Temple

Famous for its stone chariot and musical pillars, showcasing the architectural brilliance of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Hampi Bazaar & Tungabhadra

Walk through ancient bazaars and enjoy stunning views along the Tungabhadra River.

Sandur – The Green Valley

Sandur Hills

Known as the "Oasis of Ballari," Sandur is surrounded by scenic hills, forests, and pleasant weather.

Ranganatha Swamy Temple

A historic temple reflecting the religious and cultural heritage of Sandur.

Nature & Trekking

Ideal for nature lovers, trekking enthusiasts, and those seeking calm and greenery.

Things to Do

  • Heritage walks and photography in Hampi
  • Coracle ride on the Tungabhadra River
  • Trekking and nature walks in Sandur hills
  • Explore local cuisine and culture
